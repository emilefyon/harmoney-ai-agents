# AML/CFT Regulatory Signals & Sanctions Agent
*The strict, official-sources-only feed of AML/CFT regulatory, disciplinary, judicial and sanctions signals on any legal entity or natural person — built for compliance teams that cannot afford to miss a licence action, an active designation, or an open settlement.*

---

## The compliance problem
- Today, analysts manually crawl ACPR, AMF, EBA, ESMA, FCA, BaFin, CSSF, OFAC, FinCEN, JORF and a dozen more registers across jurisdictions, then cross-check published judicial decisions and UN/EU/OFAC/HMT/SDFM lists. It is slow, fragmented, and language-bound.
- What gets missed in practice: a recent licence restriction in another EU jurisdiction, a settlement still under active monitor, a fresh structural deficiency finding buried in a sanctions commission decision, a new SDN designation between two periodic reviews.
- Consequences are direct: legal-obligation breaches if an active sanctions listing or licence withdrawal is not caught at onboarding; reputational, supervisory and contractual exposure if a counterparty is taken on while under formal regulatory action.

## What the AML/CFT Regulatory Signals & Sanctions Agent does
- Runs a strictly official-sources-only search across regulators, supervisors and judicial authorities — administrative and financial sanctions, formal notices, corrective injunctions, licence restrictions / suspensions / withdrawals, published settlements with an authority (CJIP / DPA / NPA), ongoing disciplinary procedures, AML/CFT criminal charges and convictions, asset freezes, and watchlist designations.
- Qualifies every signal by procedural status — final decision, non-final decision, ongoing procedure, or publicly relayed allegation officially acknowledged. Allegations are never silently promoted into established breaches.
- Applies clear precedence rules so the file is not double-counted: a licence withdrawal supersedes a prior suspension, multiple final sanctions supersede a single one, a final conviction supersedes a non-final one. The most severe signals — final conviction, licence withdrawal, multiple final sanctions, active sanctions designation — drive the overall risk view, with adjustments for repeated cross-jurisdiction patterns and recognised mitigants.
- Honours a configurable jurisdiction scope (France, EU, EU+UK, or Global) and a configurable look-back horizon (full history vs. 24–36 months, with permanent visibility for final convictions, licence withdrawals, active designations and active asset freezes). It also surfaces hard override flags tied to legal obligations: an active OFAC / EU / UN listing or a final AML/CFT conviction triggers a "no onboarding" or "exit / relationship review" recommendation; a licence withdrawal triggers a "no onboarding" recommendation.

## What you get
For each subject, the reviewer receives a structured compliance file containing:
- An overall risk view: risk level, recommended action (for example, no onboarding, exit / relationship review, enhanced due diligence, or proceed), the dominant category driving the assessment, and the jurisdiction scope that was applied.
- Each individual AML/CFT regulatory finding presented with: the issuing authority (e.g. ACPR, FCA, BaFin, OFAC), the jurisdiction concerned, the type of action (sanction, formal notice, licence restriction, settlement, criminal proceedings, asset freeze, watchlist designation), the procedural status (final, non-final, ongoing, or officially-relayed allegation), and the date of the decision or designation.
- The recommended action for the file as a whole, including any hard overrides tied to legal obligations.
- Supporting evidence for every finding: direct links to the official authority publication or, where relevant, to recognised business press citing the official decision, with publication dates so the reviewer can re-verify in one click.
- Where relevant, an explicit mention of unresolved cases (homonymy not lifted, jurisdiction-scope limitation, no signal found, status not determinable) — surfaced openly rather than silently dropped.

- **MLROs running periodic reviews** who must surface any new enforcement action, settlement, or designation since the last assessment.
- **Sanctions screening and EDD specialists** tracking active OFAC / EU / UN / HMT designations, asset freezes, and ongoing AML/CFT criminal proceedings on portfolio entities or related parties.

## Why you can trust the output
- **Official authority sources only** for material facts: ACPR, AMF, Tracfin, EBA, ESMA, ECB / SSM, AMLA, OFAC, FinCEN, FCA, SEC, BaFin, CSSF, FINMA, DNB, NBB, MFSA, CBI, MAS, FINTRAC, AUSTRAC, FATF / MONEYVAL, published judicial decisions, the French official journal (JORF), and the UN / EU / OFAC SDN / HMT / SDFM registers.
- **Recognised business press (Reuters, AFP, Bloomberg, FT, national reference titles) is admitted only when it explicitly cites the official decision** — never as a sole source.
- Social media, blogs, forums, unofficial aggregators and unofficial databases are excluded by construction. This is what separates this agent from broader negative news screening.
- **Procedural-status discipline is enforced**: an officially-relayed allegation cannot be converted into an established breach, and the same case republished across multiple outlets counts as one signal — not many.
- **Same subject, same inputs, same answer.** The agent is built to be repeatable: two reviewers running the same file get the same risk view and the same recommended action, with a transparent breakdown of which signals drove the score and which mutual-exclusivity rules were applied.
- **Decision support, not automated verdict.** The human compliance officer always remains the final decision-maker.
