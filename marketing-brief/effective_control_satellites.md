# Effective Control Satellites — Contradictory KYB Agent
*Surfaces the people who actually steer a target entity — country MDs, BU heads, JV partners, shadow directors — who fall outside the >25% UBO net and rarely show up in any registry. Built to complement UBO mapping, not duplicate it.*

---

## The compliance problem
- Legal registers and UBO declarations only capture direct/indirect >25% holders and formally appointed mandataires. They systematically miss the people who actually run the business: country managing directors, group function heads (CFO, CCO, general counsel) operating across subsidiaries, JV partners with veto rights, and active founders who quietly retain decisional weight without a mandate.
- Regulators (ACPR, AMF, AFA, EBA guidance on EDD) increasingly expect institutions to look beyond declared mandataires and identify de facto control — especially in multi-jurisdiction groups, holdings, and partnership structures.
- Onboarding a counterparty whose real decision-maker turns out to be a sanctioned individual, a PEP, or the subject of a corruption investigation is a regulatory and reputational event — even when every box on the UBO form was ticked.

## What Effective Control Satellites does
- Searches official primary sources first (corporate sites, governance and leadership pages, appointment / M&A / reorganisation press releases, URDs, prospectuses, AMF / SEC / BaFin filings, BOAMP, official registries) and corroborates with recognised business press (Les Echos, Le Monde, FT, Bloomberg, Reuters, AFP).
- Applies a strict priority order: country directors and holding-level controllers first, then business-unit heads and holders of delegated authority, then shadow directors and strategic JV partners. A documented exception captures country directors and direct-parent holdings ended less than 24 months ago, treated as historical continuity.
- Scores every candidate on a transparent confidence grade across four dimensions — source quality, role clarity, link to the target, and recency of the role — and only surfaces candidates rated high or medium with meaningful relevance.
- Tags each surfaced satellite with a category, a factual role description, a registry-coverage check (already in declared mandataires, gap, partial match, or conflict), an AML/corruption signal flag, and an explicit "to be confirmed by a human reviewer" status.

## Hard exclusions
- **The >25% rule** — any natural or legal person with documented direct/indirect >25% capital or voting rights is presumed registry-covered and is never surfaced as a satellite.
- **The CLIENT rule** — clients of the target (users of its products or services) are never satellites, even when cited in case studies, testimonials, or commercial references.

## Risk coverage at a glance
Group & Holding Control Structure, Shadow Direction & De Facto Management, Delegated Authority & Operational Control, Strategic Partnership & Joint Venture Influence, Historical Control & Structural Continuity, Beneficial Ownership Gap & Registry Discrepancy, Public Adverse Signal on Satellite Entity, Cross-Entity Role Overlap (Satellite Dimension).

## What you get
The reviewer receives a concise list of surfaced satellite candidates. Each candidate comes with a name, a factual role description on the target, a confidence band (high, medium or low), a registry-coverage check showing whether the person is already in the declared mandataires, represents a gap, or actively conflicts with declared governance, an AML / corruption signal flag, and an explicit "to be confirmed by a human reviewer" status. Every candidate is supported by its underlying primary and press sources, with direct links and publication dates so the analyst can audit each finding in seconds. The agent never returns a UBO conclusion and never issues a legal qualification of beneficial ownership or legal representation.

## Why you can trust the output
- Evidence comes only from official primary sources (corporate sites, registries, regulator filings) or recognised business press — never social media, forums, blogs, or anonymous content.
- LinkedIn is used strictly as a pointer to confirm where to look, never as proof of a role on its own.
- Every candidate is graded on a transparent confidence score combining source quality, role clarity, link to the target, and recency of the role — with explicit guardrails against nominal titles and passive holdings being mistaken for control.
- Identical inputs produce the same findings: results are stable, traceable, and fully reviewable rather than opinion-driven.
- Every satellite candidate is tagged "to confirm" — never a UBO conclusion, never a legal qualification of beneficial ownership or legal representation.
- Human-in-the-loop by design: decision support for a contradictory KYB review, not an automated verdict.
