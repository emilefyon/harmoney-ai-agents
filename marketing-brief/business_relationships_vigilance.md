# Business Relationships & Value Chain Vigilance Agent
*Maps a legal entity's financial, supplier, distribution and tech-infrastructure partners — and surfaces the AML/CFT risk hidden in its value chain.*

---

## The compliance problem
- Counterparty risk does not stop at the entity itself: a clean legal person can still be exposed through its acquiring bank, payment service provider, hosting provider, sole supplier, or distribution chain — and reviewers rarely have time to map all of it manually.
- Legal notices, terms and conditions, payment pages, vendor disclosures and registry filings are scattered across jurisdictions and languages; high-risk-jurisdiction exposure, sensitive-sector dependencies, and sanctions-adjacent partners routinely slip through standard KYB.
- Under FATF, EBA and national AML guidance, failure to identify facilitation and value-chain exposure is a documented EDD gap — and a recurring finding in regulatory inspections.

## What the Business Relationships & Value Chain Vigilance Agent does
- Confirms the entity in official registries (SIREN, RCS, Companies House, Handelsregister, KvK, BCE, RCSL, OpenCorporates) before any partner mapping — if the entity cannot be locked to an official record, the review stops there.
- Builds a partner perimeter from admissible sources only (legal notice, T&Cs, payment pages, technical infrastructure footprint, public contracts, registry-backed references) and qualifies every link as confirmed, probable or unverified.
- Detects value-chain risk across financial rails (payment institutions, e-money issuers, acquirers, correspondent banks, crypto and virtual-asset providers), sensitive sectors, offshore and FATF-listed jurisdictions, sanctioned counterparties, and facilitation patterns (trade-based money laundering, nominee or front structures, anonymisation routing).
- Produces a structured review covering the partner relationship map, the high-risk entities identified, the distinct risk signals, complicity indicators, recommended controls, and an explicit statement of what could not be verified.

## Risk coverage at a glance
Official Partner Identification & Relationship Qualification, Financial Partners & Payment Rails, Suppliers / Subcontractors & Service Providers, Distributors / Resellers & Commercial Intermediaries, Digital Platforms / Hosting & Technical Infrastructure, Sensitive Sectors & Controlled Activities, High-Risk Jurisdictions & Offshore Exposure, Sanctions / Enforcement & Public Adverse Mentions, Opacity / Traceability Gaps & Unverifiable Relationships, Complicity & Facilitation Risk Indicators, Risk Assessment & Traceability Limits.

## What you get
The reviewer receives a structured dossier built around the entity's partner perimeter. For each finding: the risk category, an intensity (weak or strong), a confidence level, a plain-language explanation written for a compliance reader, and the underlying evidence — source name, publication date, and a direct link to the original record (never a generic homepage). Alongside the findings, the dossier sets out the partner relationship map, the high-risk entities identified, the recommended controls, and an explicit traceability-limits section listing what could not be confirmed and why. Nothing is asserted without a citation.

## Built for
- KYB / EDD analysts onboarding a corporate client whose payment rails or supplier base are not transparent.
- TPRM and vendor-risk teams screening a critical supplier or outsourced operator before contract renewal.
- MLRO / Compliance Officers running periodic review on a flagged file or preparing a SAR-adjacent investigation.

## Why you can trust the output
- Evidence is drawn only from primary registries, official sanctions lists (EU, UN, OFAC, HMT) and tier-1 press (Reuters, AFP, Bloomberg, FT, Les Echos, Le Monde). Registry-backed aggregators are accepted only when cross-referenced against a primary source.
- Social media, blogs, forums, promotional content and anonymous material are explicitly excluded as evidence.
- Every material statement cites its source, with a direct link to the original record and the publication date.
- Fully reproducible: two analysts running the same review on the same day get the same answer, and every finding can be traced line by line back to its source.
- Human-in-the-loop by design: the agent is decision support for a compliance reviewer, never an automated compliance verdict. A qualified human always has the final say.
