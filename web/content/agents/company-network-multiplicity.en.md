# Company Network Multiplicity Vigilance Agent
*Maps the full corporate web around a director or recurrent corporate actor and flags shell-pattern, mass-domiciliation, and opaque-control structures the eye misses.*

---

## The compliance problem
- Mapping a director's mandates, co-directors, shared addresses, and ownership cascades by hand across INPI, RNE, BODACC, INSEE and foreign registries takes hours per file — and analysts still stop at the first holding layer.
- Shell-company patterns, abnormal creation/cessation cycles, mass-domiciliation clusters and corporate-director chains routinely slip through onboarding because no single data source surfaces them; aggregator data alone is not admissible evidence.
- Missing a directorship ban (interdiction de gérer), an offshore corporate layer, or a circular ownership loop on a KYB file exposes the institution to LCB-FT findings, supervisory remediation (ACPR, AMF, Tracfin), and reputational fallout.

## What the Company Network Multiplicity Agent does
- Disambiguates the director's identity as a blocking first step, then attaches companies only on confirmed director-plus-company-plus-SIREN evidence — never on name alone.
- Maps confirmed mandates, ownership chains, co-directors, registered addresses, lifecycle events, sector dispersion (NACE-2), intragroup flows and BODACC proceedings into a structured network view, with density indicators that highlight unusual concentration.
- Applies a recursive corporate-director rule down to four chain levels to expose corporate directors, offshore layers, and circular ownership — patterns invisible to flat KYB checks.
- Converts observations into scored signals using closed quantitative thresholds: for example, more than 15 simultaneous mandates, six or more companies sharing one address, five or more dissolutions in 12 months, three or more shell indicators on one entity, five or more holding layers, or any offshore corporate director in the chain.

## Risk coverage at a glance
Multiple Companies, Recurring Addresses / Shared Registered Office, Recurring Co-Directors, Abnormal Creation/Cessation Cycles, Incoherent Sectors / NAF Codes, Shell Company Indicators, Control Structure & Shareholding Chain, Observable Intragroup Financial Flows, Public Mentions & Registry Proceedings, Limits & Homonymy, Traceability & Audit.

## What you get
For each finding, the reviewer sees: the risk category, an intensity rating (weak or strong), a confidence level, the quantitative basis that triggered it (such as the number of mandates, the size of the address cluster, or the depth of the ownership chain), a plain-language explanation, and the underlying evidence sources with their publication or access dates and direct links to the original registry record.

Alongside the individual findings, the file includes a full corporate-network view of the director (mandates, co-directors, addresses, ownership cascades, intragroup links), a chronological timeline of the entities' lifecycle events, an overall risk assessment with mitigating factors and Enhanced Due Diligence triggers, and a complete source list — every material statement traceable to its origin.

## Built for
- **MLROs and KYB analysts** running onboarding on a new corporate client whose UBO appears on multiple boards.
- **EDD teams** triggered by a flagged director, a BODACC liquidation, or a suspected mass-domiciliation address.
- **Periodic-review and TPRM officers** who need to detect newly created satellites, post-ban activity, or sector drift across an existing book.

## Why you can trust the output
- Evidence is drawn only from primary registries (INPI/RNE, BODACC, INSEE/data.gouv.fr) and official foreign equivalents (Companies House, Handelsregister, Registro Mercantil, KvK, BCE); commercial aggregators (Pappers, Societe.com, Infogreffe) are admissible only when corroborated by an official registry.
- No social media, forums, blogs, promotional content, or anonymous sources are ever used.
- Every material statement is cited with source name, direct link to the original record, and access date; divergences between aggregators and registries are flagged, never silently merged.
- Fully reproducible: two analysts running the same review on the same day get the same finding, traceable line by line back to the underlying evidence.
- Human-in-the-loop by design: the agent surfaces evidence and a closed-grid score; the compliance officer always makes the final decision.
