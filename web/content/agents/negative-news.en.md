# Negative News & Adverse Intelligence Agent
*Exhaustive adverse-intelligence screening on any natural person or legal entity, across 14 risk domains, with every fact qualified by procedural status — built for compliance teams who need defensible answers, not press clippings.*

---

## The compliance problem
- Analysts spend hours trawling sanctions registries, regulator notices, judicial decisions, BODACC, FATF reports and tier-1 press across multiple jurisdictions and languages — then attempt to reconcile contradictory accounts of the same case.
- Critical distinctions get lost: allegations are read as guilt, settlements (CJIP / DPA / NPA) are confused with final convictions, foreign-language primary sources are skipped, and historical sanctions designations are conflated with active ones.
- The downstream cost is asymmetric: false positives kill legitimate business and waste EDD budget; false negatives onboard a sanctioned entity, a convicted trafficker, or a subject under active criminal proceedings — with direct legal-obligation consequences for the institution.

## What the Negative News & Adverse Intelligence Agent does
- Runs an exhaustive search across the 14 covered domains (financial crime, sanctions, terrorism, trafficking, corruption, tax fraud, cybercrime, environmental, child protection and more) using only admissible primary and corroborated secondary sources.
- Qualifies every single fact with its procedural status — final conviction, non-final outcome (settlement, plea agreement, decision under appeal), ongoing proceeding (formal investigation, prosecution, dawn raid) or public allegation — and never converts an allegation into an established breach.
- Applies a closed, consistent scoring grid: a final conviction always supersedes an ongoing case or allegation on the same matter, an active sanction always supersedes a historical one, and the dominant signal drives the verdict rather than a noisy aggregate.
- Produces a single defensible verdict per subject, with the recommended action, the enhanced-due-diligence triggers, and the legal-obligation overrides clearly surfaced for the reviewer.

## Configurable scope
- **Jurisdiction scope** — France only, EU, EU + UK, or Global. Each level engages the right authorities: ACPR, AMF, PNF, Tracfin at the French level; EBA, ESMA, AMLA, OLAF, EPPO at the EU level; FCA, SFO, NCA, HMT for the UK; DOJ, SEC, OFAC, FinCEN, FATF, MONEYVAL, Interpol and Europol at the global level.
- **Monitoring mode** — full 15-year horizon for onboarding and enhanced due diligence, or a focused 24–36 month window for periodic review. Permanent exceptions remain in scope at all times: final criminal convictions, active sanctions designations, active asset freezes, and settlements still under judicial monitoring.

## What you get
For each subject, the reviewer receives a structured adverse-findings file. Every distinct finding is presented with:
- the issuing authority (regulator, prosecutor, court, sanctions body),
- the jurisdiction and the date,
- the procedural status in plain language — final conviction, non-final outcome, ongoing proceeding, or public allegation,
- a factual explanation of who, what, where, when and the reported consequences, with no legal conclusion drawn beyond the public record,
- the underlying sources cited individually, with direct links and publication dates so the analyst can open the primary document in one click.

The file also carries an overall risk level, the recommended action (standard, enhanced due diligence, escalation, no-onboarding, exit review), and any legal-obligation override that applies — for example, an active sanctions designation triggering a mandatory no-onboarding or exit decision, or a terrorism or child-protection conviction triggering mandatory escalation.

## Why you can trust the output
- Evidence is drawn only from sanctions registries, regulator communications, official judicial decisions, FATF / MONEYVAL / GRECO reports, BODACC / JORF, and tier-1 wire and business press that explicitly cites an official source.
- Social media, blogs, forums, gossip press, anonymous content and non-editorialised aggregators are excluded by design. ICIJ / OCCRP leaks are admissible only when corroborated by a primary source, and never weighed above the level of a public allegation.
- Procedural status is enforced end-to-end: allegations are never silently promoted to established breaches, and identity disambiguation (homonymy) is resolved before any substantive scoring — if it cannot be resolved, the verdict is flagged as degraded rather than guessed.
- The same subject screened twice yields the same verdict: scoring follows a closed, auditable grid with mutual exclusivity rules, dominant-signal logic, increments, mitigants and floors all traceable in the file.
- Legal-obligation overrides — active sanctions, terrorism convictions, child-protection convictions — are surfaced unambiguously as no-onboarding or exit-relationship-review. The agent always supports the reviewer's decision; it never replaces it.
