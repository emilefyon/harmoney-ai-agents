# PM Activity & Economic Substance Vigilance Agent
*Detects shell, ephemeral and no-substance legal entities — built for KYB analysts, EDD specialists and MLROs who need to separate a real operating company from a front, a coquille, or a vehicle that was created to disappear.*

---

## The compliance problem

- Manually reconstructing an entity's lifecycle means jumping between Kbis, BODACC notices, INPI filings, greffe data, foreign equivalents (Companies House, Handelsregister, KvK, BCE) and aggregator dashboards — then verifying that filed accounts, employees, an active establishment and a credible commercial trace actually exist.
- What gets missed: a company that looks "active" in an aggregator but has never filed accounts; a six-figure capital injection followed by dissolution nine months later; a serial incorporator spinning up three near-identical entities in a year; a foreign holding with no demonstrable substance in its registered jurisdiction.
- The downstream risk is well-documented in Tracfin, GAFI/FATF and parliamentary AML reports: front companies, VAT carousels, public-procurement fraud, and ephemeral vehicles used as transaction shells. Missing them at onboarding or periodic review is a direct LCB-FT exposure.

## What the PM Activity & Economic Substance Agent does

- Reconstructs the full lifecycle (incorporation, statutory modifications, dissolution, liquidation, radiation), maps filing continuity, capital and ownership history, and tests for verifiable substance: employees, active establishment, commercial trace, and cross-border footprint where relevant.
- Surfaces the patterns that matter for shell and ephemeral-entity risk: very short lifecycle, immediate dissolution, capital injected then rapidly dissolved, accounts never filed, significant filing gaps, convergence of multiple shell indicators, documented AML typology match, offshore links, foreign entities with no demonstrable local substance, and capital anomalies. Each pattern is tied to a transparent, pre-defined scoring grid so two reviewers looking at the same evidence reach the same conclusion.
- Applies structural-case exemptions for legitimate setups — pure holding companies, pre-revenue startups, project-specific SPVs and seasonal-activity businesses — so a genuine NAF 6420Z holding, an early-stage startup or a project vehicle is not flagged as a shell. Mitigating factors are applied first, hard-floors next, then the overall cap, with no double counting.

## What you get

A reviewer-ready file that reads like a structured EDD note:

- A reconstructed lifecycle timeline from creation through any corporate events to dissolution, liquidation or radiation.
- A filing-continuity assessment — are annual accounts being filed, are there unexplained gaps, has the entity ever filed at all.
- Substance indicators — evidence (or absence) of employees, an active establishment, and a credible commercial trace, including cross-border substance where the entity is a foreign holding or has foreign operations.
- Shell-pattern findings — for example, a convergence of indicators pointing to an ephemeral vehicle, an early-dissolution sequence, or a capital-injection-then-dissolution pattern, with the reasoning made explicit.
- A clear recommended action and the level of analyst attention warranted, with any structural-case exemption (holding, startup, SPV, seasonal) called out so the reviewer understands why a pattern was or was not retained.
- The full supporting evidence: every material statement carries the source name, a direct link to the original document, and the publication date of that source.

## Why you can trust the output

- Sources only from official registries (RCS, RNE, INPI, BODACC, Infogreffe, Companies House, Handelsregister, KvK, BCE, Moniteur Belge, Zefix, KRS and equivalents) and tier-1 press citing official decisions.
- Aggregator data (Pappers, Societe.com, Verif.com, Annuaire-entreprises) is admissible only when cross-referenced against the underlying official source — never on its own for a legal event, mandate, revenue figure or other statutory fact.
- Excludes blogs, social networks, forums, promotional content and non-editorialised aggregators. Offshore-leaks material (Panama, Pandora, FinCEN, ICIJ, OCCRP) is used only as a complementary signal once an official source has corroborated.
- Every fact is classified as an established official fact, a positive substance indicator, or a documented absence of evidence — and every material statement is delivered with the source name, a direct link, and the publication date.
- Same evidence in, same assessment out: the scoring grid is fixed and transparent, with the contribution of dominant findings, mitigating factors, hard-floors and the final cap visible to the reviewer. Structural-case exemptions and limited-data scenarios (unresolved homonymy, no registry data, partial gaps, foreign registry inaccessible) are stated openly rather than hidden.
- Decision support, not a verdict: the human reviewer always makes the final call. The agent never concludes to a criminal offence.
